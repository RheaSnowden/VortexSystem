
**Steam Sleeves – Detailed Design & Operation**

The steam sleeves are the high-grade energy extraction components located in the upper portion of the VORTEX tunnel. They harvest energy from the rising steam flow generated by heat dumping in the canals and basins below.

### Overview & Purpose
Steam sleeves complement the hydro sleeves by capturing kinetic energy from the upward-moving steam. They are positioned in the upper 1/3 of the tunnel where steam volume and velocity are highest. Like the hydro sleeves, they are modular and extractable for easy maintenance.

### Design Details
- **Basic form**: Cylindrical sleeve with internal guiding fins or blades optimized for steam flow (lower density than water, so fins are typically larger or more open).
- **Mounting**: Built into cross-support frames that span the tunnel. The sleeve is held in place with magnetic rings for quick release.
- **Drive system**: Vertical driveshaft running upward to a generator room above the tunnel. Multiple sleeves can share a single vertical shaft via couplings or gearboxes.
- **Magnetic coupling**: Similar to hydro sleeves — rear stabilizing ring and front disengageable ring for extraction.
- **Extractability**: Locking the rotation, disengaging the magnetic rings, and pulling the sleeve out via rail or lift system. Replacement is the reverse process. Other sleeves on the same shaft can continue operating if decoupled.

### Flow Interaction & Anticipated Effects
- Steam generated in the lower canals and basins rises into the open middle and upper tunnel.
- Guiding fins or shaped supports direct steam flow into the sleeves for efficient energy extraction.
- The open middle section allows steam volume to expand and flow naturally upward, with the hottest steam traveling along the sloped roof.
- Vertical placement takes advantage of natural convection and pressure differences created by condensation at the upper reservoir.

### Options & Variants
- **Number per section**: 4 smaller sleeves or fewer larger ones per cross-frame, depending on steam volume.
- **Fin geometry**: Looser spirals for high-volume, lower-velocity steam; adjustable or replaceable fins for optimization.
- **Coupling**: Direct vertical shaft or geared systems. Magnetic decoupling allows individual sleeve isolation.
- **Materials**: High-temperature resistant, corrosion-resistant alloys or composites suitable for wet steam environments.

### Generator Connection Arrangements
- Vertical driveshaft through the tunnel roof into an isolated generator room.
- Multiple sleeves per shaft with independent decoupling for maintenance.
- Gearboxes or variable transmission to match steam sleeve RPM to optimal generator speed.

### Projected Performance
Steam sleeves add significant power in high-heat-input scenarios. Performance depends on steam volume, velocity, temperature, and pressure — exact output requires modeling, but they are expected to contribute meaningfully to overall tunnel generation, especially when combined with hydro sleeves and high-grade heat sources.

### Maintenance & Modularity
- Quick extraction via magnetic rings and rail/lift systems.
- Generator rooms and vertical shafts are accessible from maintenance paths.
- Individual sleeves can be serviced while others remain operational.

### Integration with Tunnel & Cascade System
- Positioned in the upper tunnel where steam concentration is highest.
- Benefits from the vertical temperature gradient (hotter steam near the roof) and the open middle volume for flow.
- Works in tandem with the hydro sleeves and stepped basins that generate the steam below.

### Considerations & Limitations
- Steam is lower density than water, so sleeve design must account for reduced torque per unit volume.
- High-temperature and wet-steam corrosion resistance is critical.
- Pressure management and condensation control affect performance.
- CFD modeling of steam flow dynamics is strongly recommended.

The steam sleeves extend the VORTEX system’s energy harvesting into the vapor phase, creating a true multi-phase extraction capability within a single tunnel.
