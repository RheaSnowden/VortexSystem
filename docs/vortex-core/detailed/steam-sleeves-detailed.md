**Steam Sleeves – Detailed Design & Operation**
---

The steam sleeves are the high-grade energy extraction components located in the upper portion of the VORTEX tunnel. They harvest energy from the rising steam flow generated by heat dumping in the canals and basins below, complementing the hydro sleeves in a true dual-phase system.

### Overview & Purpose
Steam sleeves are modular and extractable for easy maintenance. They are positioned where steam volume and velocity are highest, taking advantage of natural convection and the vertical temperature gradient.

### Design Details
- **Basic form**: Cylinder-in-frame design with internal guiding fins or blades optimized for lower-density steam (typically larger/more open than hydro fins).
- **Mounting**: Around center support pylons or cross-frames in the upper tunnel. Magnetic retention rings for quick release.
- **Drive system**: Vertical or angled driveshafts to generators above or beside the tunnel, with 90° adapters as needed. Multiple sleeves can share shafts via couplings or gearboxes.
- **Magnetic coupling**: Similar to hydro sleeves — stabilizing rings and disengageable front ring for extraction.
- **Extractability**: Lock rotation, disengage magnets, pull via rail/lift system. Other sleeves on the same shaft can continue operating if decoupled.

### Flow Interaction & Anticipated Effects
- Guiding partitions and fins direct rising steam (from thermosiphon + condensation vacuum) into the sleeves.
- Open middle section allows steam to expand and flow naturally upward, with hottest steam near the roof.
- Handles two-phase (wet) steam with drainage features and corrosion-resistant materials.
- Optional stator vanes and converging outlets for efficiency.

### Options & Variants
- Number per section (2–4 smaller sleeves recommended for distribution and maintenance).
- Fin/blade geometry: Airfoil or gradient shapes; swappable cartridges for optimization.
- Coupling and mounting: Flexible configurations around supports.
- Materials: High-temperature, wet-steam resistant alloys or composites.

### Generator Connection Arrangements
- Vertical driveshafts through the tunnel roof into isolated generator rooms.
- Independent decoupling for partial maintenance.
- Gearboxes to match RPM to optimal generator speed.

### Projected Performance & Considerations
Steam sleeves add meaningful power in high-heat scenarios, especially with strong thermosiphon drive. Performance depends on steam volume, velocity, temperature, and pressure. CFD modeling of steam flow dynamics is strongly recommended. This leverages proven axial steam turbine technology adapted to the modular, distributed VORTEX philosophy.

### Maintenance & Modularity
- Quick extraction via magnetic rings and rail/lift systems.
- Individual sleeves can be serviced while others remain operational.
- Generator rooms accessible from maintenance paths.

### Integration with Tunnel & Cascade System
- Works in tandem with hydro sleeves and stepped basins that generate the steam.
- Benefits from the open middle volume and vertical temperature gradient.
