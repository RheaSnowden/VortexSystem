# How It Works

VORTEX is a modular inclined tunnel system that turns waste heat into electricity while enabling closed-loop thermal management.

## Core Operating Principle
The system uses a hybrid liquid/steam thermosiphon + gravity-driven flow in inclined tunnels (30–45° slope, 30° preferred for length). Heat added at the lower end evaporates water → steam rises buoyantly → condenses higher up → liquid water flows downhill by gravity. Both phases drive power generation.

This creates a self-sustaining loop once primed. Continuous running leads to heat saturation, which extends hot zones and reduces the marginal heat needed to maintain flow.

## Main Components & Operation
- **Inclined Tunnels**: Straight, oval loops, or stacked spirals. Utility corridors house thermal fluid piping.
- **Stepped Basins**: Heat exchange, level control, and localized evaporation. Heat is delivered from the cascading thermal loop.
- **Hydro Sleeves** (lower sections): Custom or proven watermill-style units extract energy from downward liquid flow. Modular and quick-swap.
- **Steam Sleeves** (upper sections): Extract kinetic energy from rising vapor. Can use custom sleeves or conventional low-pressure steam turbines in a proof-of-concept variant.
- **Maintenance**: Rail + crane system for rapid sleeve and component swaps without full shutdown.

## Dual-Phase Energy Harvesting
- **Downward liquid flow** drives hydro harvesting (gravity + flow momentum).
- **Upward steam flow** drives steam harvesting (kinetic energy + pressure/volume expansion).
- The shared driveshaft or individual generators convert mechanical rotation to electricity.

In looped configurations a central shared thermal reservoir feeds the loop. Most water evaporates before reaching the lowest point (“endless waterfall” mode), minimizing upward pumping.
