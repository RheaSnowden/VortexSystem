# Power Sources & Integration

VORTEX is heat-source agnostic and works with nuclear, coal, industrial waste heat, solar+batteries, and more. It captures both high-grade steam and lower-grade waste heat.

### Nuclear Integration
Ideal anchor. A central nuclear plant can feed steam and waste heat into surrounding VORTEX rings or tunnels, significantly boosting overall efficiency.

### Coal & Industrial Waste Heat
Viable with scrubbing and heat recovery. Captured CO₂ can feed carbon valleys; ash can be processed into fertilizer.

### Solar + Battery Synergy
Strong pairing. Daytime excess solar charges batteries; battery discharge waste heat feeds the tunnels at night, smoothing the thermosiphon and improving capacity factor.

### Preliminary Performance Estimates
Power output depends on head, flow rate, heat input grade, and efficiency.

**Basic Formula** (simplified):  
**P ≈ ρ × g × Q × H × η**  
(where P = power, ρ = density, g = gravity, Q = flow rate, H = effective head, η = overall efficiency ~60–75% for sleeves)

**Example Ballparks** (per tunnel, strong heat input):
- 1 mile effective length: 100–800+ MW electric (hundreds of MW thermal absorption typical).
- 3+ miles or stacked/ringed: Multi-GW scale possible.
- Per 150 ft section (V-formation, 6 hydro sleeves): Roughly proportional scaling. Steam production per section supports the hydro flow; exact values depend on heat grade.

**Generator Linking Options**:
- 2–3 sleeves per generator (incremental, lower torque per unit).
- Larger linked setups (4–8+ sleeves) for cost reduction and higher efficiency per generator.

These are order-of-magnitude estimates. Real performance requires site-specific modeling. A minimum heat/steam threshold is needed to sustain natural thermosiphon without significant pumping.
