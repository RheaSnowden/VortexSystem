# Tunnel Construction & Layouts

The VORTEX tunnel is the structural and functional backbone of the system. It can be built in straight, oval/looped, or spiraled configurations depending on site needs.

### General Construction Features
- **Slope**: 30–45° for reliable gravity drive and thermosiphon (30° preferred for longer path length and heat exchange surface).
- **Cross-Section**: Divided into upper steam zone (~2/3), lower hydro zone (~1/3), and open middle mix area.
- **Center Supports**: Pylons or beams that also serve as mounting points for steam sleeves and flow-directing partitions.
- **Walls & Utility Corridors**: Thick, insulated construction with dedicated spaces for piping, driveshafts, access, and maintenance rails.
- **Material Considerations**: Reinforced concrete or composite sections with internal liners for corrosion/erosion resistance.

### Layout Variants
**Straight Tunnels**
- Ideal for long corridors and multi-source heat collection.
- Simplest construction and maintenance.

**Oval / Stadium-Style Loops**
- Compact footprint with good total length.
- Excellent for industrial parks and arcology perimeters.

**Stacked Spirals / "Spiraled Walls"**
- Dense vertical integration for arcologies.
- Maximizes capacity within limited horizontal space.

**Hybrid & Branching**
- Mix of straight and curved sections for redundancy and expansion.

### Construction Phasing & Benefits
- Can be built incrementally — early sections help power further construction.
- Adapts to land, topography, and arcology geometry.
- Supports both compact high-density and long linear deployments.
