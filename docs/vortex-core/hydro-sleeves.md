# Hydro Sleeves

Hydro sleeves extract energy from the downward liquid flow in the lower tunnel sections. They are one of the primary power-generating components of the VORTEX system.

### Design Overview
- Horizontal orientation (level) in the 30–45° sloped tunnel for optimal flow capture and drive shaft routing.
- Gradient spiral/helical fins along the inner cylinder wall (visualization inspiration: wall-mounted helical guiding fins in a tapering cylinder).
- Inlet wider (100%), tapering to ~75% at the exit for velocity increase and balanced torque.
- Open-center rotating cylinder with rim drive (cog or magnetic coupling).
- Jet exit with air gap before landing in the overflow return or basin for reduced erosion.

### Paired Sleeve Layout
- Preferred configuration: 2 hydro sleeves per shared drive shaft (counter-rotating for torque balance and stability), with 4 sleeves per 150 ft module integrated with the canal system.
- This improves mechanical simplicity while maintaining good flow distribution.

### Drive & Generator Integration
- Horizontal drive shafts drop through the floor into protected generator rooms or run in a parallel dry gallery.
- Selective linking of paired driveshafts to shared generators via external connecting shafts or gearboxes in utility corridors.
- Magnetic rim drive + radial/axial centering (SmCo magnets for high-temp tolerance) preferred for low friction and easy maintenance. Hybrid (magnetic + backup bearings) for safety.
- Forward thrust cancellation via a dedicated ring/magnet setup at the inlet.

### Operation & Efficiency
- Water enters via gradual funnel → full enclosure with pre-rotation grooves.
- Rotation driven by flow momentum and gravity.
- Exit jet has air time before cushioning in overflow or basin (accounting for the horizontal sleeve in the continuing 30° canal slope).
- Modular and extractable via rail + crane system (target: 10–30 minute swaps).

### Benefits
- High efficiency in channeled low-head flow.
- Distributed extraction reduces single-point failure risk.
- Excellent maintainability and scalability.
- This is **one feasible open-flow turbine concept**; fin geometry, taper ratio, grouping, and materials can be adapted to specific sites and flow conditions.
