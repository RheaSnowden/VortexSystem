# Hydro Sleeves

Hydro sleeves extract energy from the downward liquid flow in the lower tunnel sections. They are one of the primary power-generating components of the VORTEX system.

### Design Overview
- Horizontal orientation (level) in the 30–45° sloped tunnel for optimal flow capture and drive shaft routing.
- Gradient spiral/helical fins along the inner cylinder wall.
- Inlet wider (100%), tapering to ~75% at the exit for velocity increase and balanced torque.
- Open-center rotating cylinder with rim drive (cog or magnetic coupling).
- Jet exit with air gap before landing in the overflow return or basin for reduced erosion.

### V-Formation Layout
- 3 sleeves per driveshaft in staggered V-formation (6 sleeves per 150 ft section, mirrored on both halves of the tunnel).
- Each sleeve has its own dedicated main canal.
- Improves density, torque balance, and clearance while maintaining good flow distribution.

### Drive & Generator Integration
- Horizontal drive shafts drop through the floor into protected generator rooms.
- Selective linking of 2–4 paired driveshafts to shared generators via external connecting shafts in utility corridors.
- Magnetic rim drive + radial/axial centering (SmCo magnets for high-temp tolerance) preferred for low friction and easy maintenance. Hybrid (magnetic + backup bearings) for safety.
- Gearboxes and flexible couplings manage accumulated torque.

### Operation & Efficiency
- Water enters via gradual funnel → full enclosure with pre-rotation grooves.
- Rotation driven by flow momentum and gravity.
- Exit jet has air time before cushioning in overflow or basin.
- Modular and extractable via rail + crane system (target: 10–30 minute swaps).

### Benefits
- High efficiency in channeled flow.
- Distributed extraction reduces single-point failure risk.
- Easy maintenance and scalability.
