# Steam Sleeves

Steam sleeves extract energy from the upward vapor/steam flow in the upper section of the tunnel. They complement the hydro sleeves to create a dual-phase harvesting system.

### Design Overview
- Mounted in the upper ~2/3 of the tunnel around center support pylons or cross-frames.
- Flow-directing partitions and guiding fins in the open middle direct steam into the sleeves.
- Modular cylinder-in-frame design with magnetic retention rings for easy extraction.
- Rim drive (cog or magnetic coupling) to external driveshaft/generator.
- Vertical or angled driveshaft routing (depending on slope and generator placement) with 90° adapters where needed.

### Recommended Configuration
Multiple smaller sleeves (e.g., 2–4 per section) are preferred over fewer large units for:
- Better distribution along the tunnel length.
- Easier individual extraction and partial operation during maintenance.
- Improved redundancy and granular control.
- Lighter, more manageable units for the ceiling lift system.

### Internal Flow Path
The internals draw from proven axial steam turbine and ducted impeller technology:
- Gradient or airfoil-shaped fins/blades optimized for steam velocity and density.
- Optional stator/guide vanes downstream of the rotating element to recover swirl energy and improve efficiency.
- Proper tip clearance and hub design to minimize losses.
- Optional converging outlet section for better pressure recovery.

Swappable internal cartridges can be used within the same outer housing for site-specific optimization or future upgrades.

### Operation
Rising steam (driven by thermosiphon and condensation vacuum) passes through the sleeves. Kinetic energy is converted to mechanical rotation and then electricity. The sleeves handle the two-phase nature of the flow (wet steam) with appropriate materials and drainage features.

### Benefits
- High availability — individual sleeves can be serviced without stopping the entire tunnel.
- Efficient harvesting from the upward flow.
- Modular and adaptable to different steam conditions.
- Strong synergy with the overall distributed philosophy of VORTEX.
